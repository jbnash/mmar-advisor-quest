How to Play
The MMAR Model
Your students use Mixed Methods Action Research — a six-phase iterative cycle. Unlike PhD programs, they build their dissertation during coursework. Each course scaffolds a specific phase.
1Diagnosis — Using data to understand what's really happening in the organization 2Reconnaissance — Collecting data to identify root causes and inform action 3Planning — Designing an intervention grounded in what was learned 4Acting — Implementing the intervention in the real organizational context 5Evaluation — Did the intervention make a difference? Qual + quant evidence 6Reflection — What worked, what didn't, implications for future cycles
Scenario Sprint
Read the student situation carefully. All four answers may seem plausible — you're looking for the most important first move. Select your answer, then read the feedback to understand the reasoning. 20 XP per correct answer, 50 XP bonus for a perfect round.
Case File Escape
Click all clue cards first before answering — the evidence matters. Each correct phase decision unlocks the next phase (40 XP). Wrong answers move you forward too, with an explanation. Completing the full case earns a 100 XP bonus.
A Note on the Answers
These scenarios don't have one objectively correct answer — they have a best first move given the specific context. If you'd argue for a different choice, that instinct is worth examining. The feedback explains the reasoning, not the rule.
